Entrance Exam Process: Conduct An Entrance Exam

Entrance Exams are conducted by many educational institutions to select students for admissions into a particular stream, class or honor. Capability of students in various fields is evaluated through these entrance exams. These exams which are very competitive in nature teach students to deal with competition they are about to face in their future. In India, entrance exams for various national level and state level entrance exams are conducted for admissions into various streams like management, medical, engineering, law, finance & accounts, hospitality, arts & design, government services, information and technology etc. Students who crack these exams are selected in the top colleges. We provide you with detailed information about each exam in India which includes general information about the exam, eligibility criteria, important dates, syllabus, preparation tips and much more.

Type of Entrance Exams in India

Chiefly, entrance exams can be divided into 3 broad categories, which are:

  • National Level Entrance Exams / All India Level Entrance Exams.
  • State Level Entrance Exams.
  • University / Institute Level Entrance Exam.
  • National Level Entrance Exams / All India Level Entrance Exams – These are those examination, which are conducted throughout the nation and candidates from all over India can apply for it. The score of the exam is considered by colleges / universities / institute throughout the nation. Some of such exams with the name of the conducting body are as follows:

  • Mode of Entrance Exam in India

    Earlier, the entrance exams were held on pen and paper but as the technology advanced, the mode of examination changed. Few of the following popular modes in which the exams are held are as follows:

    Offline Mode – This is the traditional pen and paper format, in which candidates have to write answer on a paper or OMR sheet.

    CBT Mode – Compute based test mode is the most popular mode of entrance exams right now. Here, the candidates have to attempt the exam on a computer and mark answers with the help of a mouse. In some of the exams, candidates even get virtual calculate as well.

    Online Mode – Even in online mode the candidates take the exam via computer. However, the only difference is that candidates can take the exam form anywhere and are not restricted to go to a particular center. To take these types of exam, candidates just need to have a computer, good internet connection, and a webcam.
